Digital Twin of 6DOF Collaborative Robot

Robotics & Simulation

Objective & Design Requirements

Develop a comprehensive digital twin simulation of the UR5 collaborative robot to enable advanced motion planning, control optimization, and predictive maintenance capabilities for industrial automation applications.

  • Real-time kinematic and dynamic modeling of 6-DOF robotic arm
  • Implementation of smooth trajectory planning algorithms
  • Joint space and Cartesian space motion control
  • Simulation accuracy within 2% of physical system performance
  • Integration with existing industrial control systems

Technical Approach & Methodology

Utilized MATLAB Simulink and Robotics Toolbox to create comprehensive robot model including forward/inverse kinematics, dynamic equations, and control algorithms. Implemented trapezoidal velocity profiles for smooth motion planning and integrated computed torque control for precise positioning.

Specific Technical Contributions

  • Developed complete kinematic model using Denavit-Hartenberg parameters
  • Implemented trapezoidal velocity profile algorithm for smoother TCP motion
  • Created dynamic simulation including joint friction and motor characteristics
  • Designed and validated control algorithms for trajectory tracking
  • Performed singularity analysis and avoidance strategies

Technical Details

The digital twin incorporates full 6-DOF kinematics with D-H parameter modeling, joint space trajectory generation using quintic polynomials, and computed torque control with gravity compensation. Real-time simulation runs at 1kHz update rate with less than 1ms latency.

Results & Evaluation

Successfully achieved 40% reduction in joint stress through optimized velocity profiles, improved motion smoothness by eliminating velocity discontinuities, and enabled predictive maintenance through digital monitoring. Simulation accuracy validated within 1.5% of physical robot performance.

MATLAB Simulink Robotics Toolbox Kinematics Control Systems
